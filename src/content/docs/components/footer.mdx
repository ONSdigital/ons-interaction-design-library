The ONS new website footer is a consistent navigational element positioned at the base of every page. It serves as a safety net for users, providing access to essential secondary information, legal requirements, and external social channels without distracting from the main page content.

For the website variant, the footer uses a three-column layout for primary links. This structure ensures that ***Help*** and ***About*** information is easily discoverable while clearly separating external social links.

## Purpose and goals

The footer ensures that users have a predictable place to find *meta* information about the ONS and the website itself. It aims to achieve:

- **Consistency:** Meeting UK accessibility standards by providing help and legal links in a predictable location.

- **Trust:** Displaying the Open Government Licence (OGL) and copyright information to establish authority and transparency.

- **Connectivity:** Offering a dedicated space to for links to other ONS channels via external platforms.

## When to use this component

Use this component at the bottom of every page on the ONS website to provide a consistent closing experience for the user journey.

**Do not** use this component to house primary navigation or **Call to action** buttons that belong in the header or the main body of the page. Avoid adding a fourth column, as this creates visual clutter and impacts scannability.

## Structure and anatomy

The website footer variant is composed of the following distinct sections:

#### Secondary Navigation Columns

The links are organised into three distinct columns:

- **Help:** The highest priority area for users seeking support. This includes links like ***Contact us*** and ***Accessibility***.

- **About ONS:** Contains organisational links such as ***What we do*** or ***Transparency***.

- **Connect with us:** Contains links to social media and external platforms.


#### Legal and Licensing Block

Located at the base of the footer, this section contains the **Open Government Licence (OGL)** logo and the required copyright statement.

## Design Behaviour

To ensure a seamless experience across all devices, the following behaviours are required:

#### Responsive Stacking

- **Desktop/Tablet:** Links are displayed in three side-by-side columns.

- **Mobile (below 740px):** The three columns stack vertically. The ***Help*** column must remain at the top of the stack, followed by ***About ONS***, and finally ***Connect with us***.
